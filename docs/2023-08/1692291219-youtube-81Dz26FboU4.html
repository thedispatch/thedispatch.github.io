"Into the Fray: A Rare Glimpse into Norway's Elite Coastal Ranger Commando" by The Dispatch

A recently released video has given the world a rare glimpse into the secretive and elite Coastal Ranger Commando, a unit of the Norwegian Armed Forces. The unit, known for boarding suspicious ships in Norwegian waters, is responsible for one of the most dangerous military jobs in the world.

The video offers a unique perspective on the unit's grueling selection process, which is so intense that less than 1% of applicants make it through. It follows the journey of the host as he attempts to pass the unit's physical fitness tests, in a bid to join them on a mission.

The host is seen struggling with the physical tests, particularly the leg-focused exercises. The video showcases a range of demanding tests, including pull-ups, deadlifts, running, swimming, and climbing a ladder.

Despite passing the majority of the tests, the host fails to climb the ladder with the added weight of gear, demonstrating the immense physical strength required for this elite force.

In an unexpected turn, despite this failure, the Coastal Ranger Commando agrees to take the host on a real mission, without any safety measures. This decision underscores the unit's confidence in their training and their commitment to the mission, regardless of the risks involved.

This video provides a rare and insightful look into the demanding world of Norway's elite military unit, showcasing the physical and mental strength required to serve in one of the most dangerous military jobs in the world.
